Ajanta & Ellora Caves

These magnificent 34 monasteries and temples, dug into a basalt cliff, bring the civilization of ancient India to life. The Ajanta and Ellora caves are architectural marvels known for their stunning paintings, sculptures and rock-cut architecture that showcase the rich cultural history of India.

Read on Wikipedia

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves are approximately 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE. The caves include paintings and sculptures considered to be masterpieces of Buddhist religious art, depicting the Jataka tales and various incidents from the life of Buddha.

Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 30 km from the city of Aurangabad. The caves feature Buddhist, Hindu and Jain monuments, and artwork, dating from 600-1000 CE. Cave 16 features the largest single monolithic rock excavation in the world, the Kailasha temple.
