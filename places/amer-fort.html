Amer Fort

Known for its artistic style, Amer Fort overlooks Maota Lake and is a great example of Rajput architecture in Jaipur. With its large ramparts, series of gates and cobbled paths, the fort overlooks the Maota Lake, which is the main source of water for the Amer Palace.

Read on Wikipedia

History

Amer Fort was built by Raja Man Singh I in 1592 and expanded by subsequent rulers until the 18th century. The fort served as the residence of the Rajput Maharajas and their families. It was later expanded by Raja Jai Singh I, who was known for his bravery and victories in battle.

Architecture

The fort is known for its artistic Hindu style elements with large ramparts and series of gates and cobbled paths. The fort is constructed of red sandstone and marble and is laid out on four levels, each with a courtyard. It consists of the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), and Sukh Niwas.
