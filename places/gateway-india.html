Gateway of India

An arch monument built in Mumbai to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911. Located on the waterfront in the Apollo Bunder area of South Mumbai, the Gateway of India is one of the city's most iconic landmarks and a testament to British colonial architecture in India.

Read on Wikipedia

History

The Gateway of India was erected to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder, Mumbai in December 1911. Although the foundation stone was laid in 1913, the actual construction only began in 1915 and was completed in 1924. Ironically, it later became a symbol of the end of British rule when the last British troops departed through it after Indian Independence in 1947.

Architecture

Designed by architect George Wittet, the Gateway combines elements of Indo-Saracenic style with Roman triumphal arch influences. The structure is 26 meters high and built from yellow basalt and reinforced concrete. The central dome is 48 feet in diameter, and the whole monument was designed to be the first thing visitors would see when approaching Mumbai from the sea.
