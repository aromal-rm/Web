Hampi

The last capital of the Vijayanagar Empire, Hampi is known for its Dravidian temples, palaces, and stunning ruins amid a surreal landscape of boulders. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi's ruins stretch over an area of more than 25 square kilometers and represent the remnants of what was once one of the richest and largest cities in the world.

Read on Wikipedia

History

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from the 14th century until its fall in 1565. At its peak, Hampi was one of the richest and largest cities in the world, attracting many traders from Persia and Portugal. The city was conquered and destroyed by a coalition of Deccan sultanates in 1565, after which it fell into ruin and was largely abandoned.

Architecture

The ruins of Hampi represent a unique artistic creation and bear exceptional testimony to the vanished civilization of the Vijayanagara Empire. The sacred center contains the most beautiful structures, including the Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple with its famous stone chariot and musical pillars, Hazara Rama temple known for its intricate carvings, and the majestic Lotus Mahal.

Visit Information
