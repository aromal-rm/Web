Khajuraho

Famous for its temples with intricate sculptures, built during the Chandella dynasty between 950 and 1050 CE. The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its nagara-style architectural symbolism and its erotic sculptures that represent the ancient Indian approach to human sexuality as a part of human life and spiritual journey.

Read on Wikipedia

History

The Khajuraho temples were built during the Chandella dynasty, which reached its apogee between 950 and 1050 CE. Originally there were over 80 Hindu temples, of which only about 25 now survive. After the decline of the Chandella dynasty in the 13th century, the temples were abandoned and gradually fell into disuse and disrepair. They were rediscovered during the British colonial period in the late 19th century.

Architecture

The temples at Khajuraho are known for their nagara-style architectural symbolism and exquisite stone carvings. The temples are grouped into three geographical divisions: Western, Eastern and Southern. The Western Group contains the largest and most famous temples, including the Kandariya Mahadeva Temple. The sculptures depict deities, celestial nymphs, musicians, mythological scenes, and various aspects of life, including the famous erotic sculptures.
