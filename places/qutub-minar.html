Qutub Minar

The red sandstone tower of Qutb Minar is 72.5 m high, built in the early 13th century, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi. It's the tallest brick minaret in the world and an excellent example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Read on Wikipedia

History

Qutub Minar was built by Qutub-ud-Din Aibak, the first Sultan of Delhi, in 1199 AD. It was later completed by his successor Iltutmish. The tower was built to celebrate the victory of Mohammed Ghori over the Rajputs, marking the beginning of Muslim rule in India.

Architecture

The tower consists of five distinct storeys, each marked by a projecting balcony. The first three storeys are made of red sandstone, while the fourth and fifth storeys are made of marble and sandstone. The tower is covered with intricate carvings and verses from the Quran.

Visit Information
