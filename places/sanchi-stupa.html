Sanchi Stupa

The oldest Buddhist sanctuary in existence, Sanchi was a major Buddhist centre in India until the 12th century A.D. The Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India and was commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Read on Wikipedia

History

The Great Stupa at Sanchi was originally commissioned by Emperor Ashoka the Great in the 3rd century BCE to house the relics of Buddha. The original structure was a simple brick dome, which was later expanded and embellished with the characteristic features we see today, including the gateways (toranas) that were added during the Satavahana period in the 1st century BCE.

Architecture

The Sanchi Stupa is a hemispherical brick structure built over the relics of Buddha. The most striking feature of the stupa is its four ornate gateways (toranas) that face the cardinal directions. These gateways are adorned with intricate carvings depicting scenes from Buddhist mythology and the life of Buddha. The carvings also illustrate various Jataka tales, which are stories of Buddha's previous lives.
