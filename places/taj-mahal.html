Taj Mahal

An immense mausoleum of white marble, built in Agra between 1631 and 1648 by order of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favourite wife, the Taj Mahal is the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.

Read on Wikipedia

History

The Taj Mahal was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his third wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1648. It employed around 20,000 artisans under the guidance of the court architect, Ustad Ahmad Lahauri.

Architecture

The white marble mausoleum stands on a square plinth with a minaret at each corner. It combines elements from Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. The main chamber houses the cenotaphs of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal.

Visit Information

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh
Opening Hours: Sunrise to Sunset (Closed on Fridays)
Entry Fee: ₹1,100 for Foreign Tourists, ₹50 for Indian Nationals
Best Time to Visit: October to March
