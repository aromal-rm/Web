Victoria Memorial

This grand white marbled structure in Kolkata was built in memory of Queen Victoria, the Empress of India from 1876 to 1901. Now serving as a museum and tourist destination, the Victoria Memorial is an iconic symbol of the city and a beautiful example of Indo-Saracenic revivalist architecture that blends British and Mughal elements.

Read on Wikipedia

History

The Victoria Memorial was conceptualized by Lord Curzon, the Viceroy of India, after the death of Queen Victoria in 1901. Construction began in 1906 and was completed in 1921. The memorial was designed by William Emerson, a prominent British architect, and was built using white Makrana marble. It was officially opened to the public in 1921 and has since been a major landmark and museum in Kolkata.

Architecture

The Victoria Memorial is a magnificent building that blends British and Mughal architectural styles. The central dome is inspired by the Taj Mahal, while the design incorporates various elements from Egyptian, Venetian, Deccani, and Islamic architectural styles. The memorial is built of white Makrana marble and spans over 64 acres of land. The structure stands 184 feet tall and is adorned with numerous sculptures and statues.

Visit Information

Location: Kolkata, West Bengal
